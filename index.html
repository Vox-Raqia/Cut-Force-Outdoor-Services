Before You Book

A quick checklist that helps everything go smoother

A little information upfront helps make sure the job is safe, accurate, and efficient.

Here's what helps most:

1. Send a photo of the job

A clear picture saves time and gives an accurate sense of the work needed.

2. Describe access to the area

Let me know if there are:

  • gates
  • narrow paths
  • steep slopes
  • tight corners
  • limited parking

This helps plan the safest approach.

3. Mention any hazards

Examples include:

  • loose soil
  • unstable branches
  • nearby power lines
  • wasp nests
  • pets on the property

Safety comes first — for you, your property, and me.

4. Let me know if debris needs hauling

Some jobs create more material than others.
Knowing this ahead of time helps plan cleanup and disposal.

5. Share your preferred timing

If you have a specific day or window in mind, mention it.
Scheduling is flexible, but clear expectations help everything run smoothly.

Clear info means a clear quote

The more details you provide upfront, the more accurate and fair the quote will be — no surprises, no guesswork.

If anything is unclear, I'll walk you through it — no stress.

Straightforward Pricing

Fair, simple, and honest

Pricing at Cut Force Outdoor Services is built on clarity — no hidden fees, no surprise add‑ons, and no confusing packages.

Every quote is based on three things:

  1. Time
    How long the job will realistically take, including setup and cleanup.
  2. Difficulty
    Some tasks are simple.
    Some require more caution, more effort, or more precision.
    The quote reflects the actual work involved — nothing more.
  3. Disposal Needs
    If debris needs to be hauled away, the quote will include the time and effort required to load, transport, and dispose of it properly.

No upsells. No pressure. No nonsense.

You'll never be pushed into services you don't need.

If something is outside the current equipment or skill level, you'll hear that upfront — not after the job starts.

Clear quotes, clear expectations

Most small jobs fit into a few hours of work. Larger brush clearing or debris hauling can take longer — but you'll always know the expected cost before I start.
