About Cut Force Outdoor Services

Show up. Work safely. Leave every property better.

What drives the work

Cut Force Outdoor Services is a one‑person operation based in Hope, BC.

It started simple: a pickup truck, a chainsaw, and a pressure washer—plus a willingness to take on the kind of outdoor work most people don’t want to do alone.

There’s no big crew, no fleet, and no corporate office. Just local, hands‑on work done by someone who cares about doing the job properly and earning trust over time.

Insurance and additional equipment are in progress as the business grows, but the focus right now is clear: show up, work safely, and leave every property better than it was.

What drives the work
The goal isn’t to look like a big company.
The goal is to be useful.

That means:

Showing up when agreed

Communicating clearly

Being honest about what can and can’t be done

Treating every job like it actually matters

Every project is another step toward building a stronger, more capable local service.

What we do right now

Cut Force focuses on practical, small‑to‑medium outdoor jobs, including:

Chainsaw work
Limb and small tree removal, cutting downed or problem branches, and helping reduce risk around structures where it’s safe to do so.

Brush clearing for fire safety
Clearing overgrown brush, dry grass, and light debris to help reduce fire risk and make properties easier to maintain.

Pressure washing
Cleaning driveways, walkways, decks, and exterior surfaces to remove built‑up dirt, moss, and grime.

If a job requires heavy machinery, specialized rigging, or advanced arborist work, that’s outside the current scope—and you’ll be told that upfront.

Tools I Use

Simple tools. Reliable results.

Cut Force Outdoor Services runs on practical, dependable equipment — nothing flashy, nothing over‑promised. Just the right tools for the work being offered today.

Pickup Truck
The backbone of the operation.
Used for hauling debris, transporting tools, and keeping job sites clean and organized.
It’s not a commercial fleet vehicle — it’s a reliable workhorse that gets the job done.

Chainsaw
For small‑to‑medium tree and limb work.
Used with caution, respect, and a focus on safety.
Perfect for clearing branches, cutting fallen limbs, and handling manageable tree work around homes and driveways.

Pressure Washer
Ideal for cleaning driveways, walkways, decks, siding, and exterior surfaces.
Removes moss, grime, and buildup — especially useful in BC’s damp climate.
A simple tool that makes a big visual difference.

Why this matters
These tools define the scope of the work:

No industrial machinery

No heavy land‑clearing equipment

No advanced arborist rigging

No large crews

Just straightforward, honest work done with the right tools for the job.

Growing the toolkit over time
As the business expands — and insurance is finalized — more equipment will be added.
For now, the focus is on doing excellent work with the essentials.

What you can expect

Straight talk
You’ll get clear expectations about what’s possible, what it will likely cost, and how long it should take.

Safety‑minded decisions
If something looks unsafe or beyond the current setup, it won’t be guessed at or improvised. It will be flagged, not forced.

Respect for your property
The goal is to leave your place cleaner, safer, and more usable—not torn up or trashed.

Realistic results
No “instant transformation” promises. Just steady, practical improvements that make a visible difference.

What we’re not (yet)

To keep things honest and clear:

Not a large crew

Not equipped for major land clearing or hazardous tree removals

Not fully insured yet (in progress)

Not offering emergency storm response or disaster restoration

As the business grows, some of this will change. For now, the focus stays on what can be done well with the tools on hand.

Growing one job at a time

Cut Force Outdoor Services is a work in progress—on purpose.
Each job helps build experience, buy better equipment, and move closer to a fully equipped, fully insured operation serving more of the region around Hope, BC.

If you want straightforward help with outdoor work and prefer dealing directly with the person doing the job, Cut Force is built for that.
